Anxious

Anxiety is a feeling of unease, worry or fear. Everyone feels anxious at some point in their life, but there are ways to help ease your anxiety now. Here are some ways you can calm your anxiety now:

  • Take a break
  • Breathe in and out
  • Go for a walk or do something different
  • Watch an entertaining video

Frustrated

The problem with frustration—no matter in what context—is that being “upset or annoyed” adds a second layer of suffering to the emotional suffering you’re already caught up in. It clouds your mind, but here are a few ways to help overcome your frustration now:

  • Realize the feeling is not permanent
  • You are not alone, contact friends/family to vent or let out your feelings through writing or an activity
  • Recognize factors in life that are out of your control and accept them
  • Focus on only today

It’s not necessarily about being physically surrounded by people—because you might feel especially lonely in a crowd—but about your mentality. When you feel lonely, it’s usually because you aren’t quite satisfied with what you have, whether it’s in that moment or throughout your life. The upside: Feeling lonely isn't necessarily a bad thing. It's a reminder that something's off about your social environment and that you need to prioritize your happiness.

  • You are not alone with your loneliness
  • Don't deny or distance and say you do not need anyone, everyone needs contact sometimes
  • Write down positive memories
  • Join a class or club, step outside your comfort zone

Gloomy

Sometimes, we just feel gloomy — at work or at home. You may love your job and the work you do but still find yourself feeling down once in a while. It’s OK. There are other ways to cope that can help you get through the sad times.

  • Do a hobby or any activity that you enjoy
  • Listen to music
  • Take a step back and reflect on your feelings
  • Laugh or connect with someone
  • Seek help if necessary

Stressed

Stress can also help you rise to meet challenges. But beyond a certain point, stress stops being helpful and starts causing major damage to your health, mood, productivity, relationships, and your quality of life. Here are some steps that you can take to reduce its harmful effects.

  • Organize, it'll help you feel in control and feel accomplished
  • Meditate
  • Exercise, even if its just walking around
  • Talk it out with someone or write about it

Lethargic

It’s okay if you didn’t finish your homework, or couldn’t send out those emails for work, or just could not bring yourself to climb out of bed. Sometimes we are tired and it feels like the world speeds by around you while you’re stuck in the doldrums. If this happens consistently and interferes with your day-to-day life, go see your doctor to make sure it’s not a symptom of a more severe medical issue. But if it’s just one of those bad days, here are some ways to get you out of that slump.

  • Make sure you’re staying hydrated. Water is vital!
  • If you don’t have the energy to go for a jog, try a stroll around the block. Even standing or sitting outside if it’s sunny or pleasant can help boost your mood and energy.
  • Have a fruit or vegetable. Cooking a meal may be too much effort right now, but grabbing a fresh fruit or vegetable, like a carrot or cucumber, can fuel your body and mind. Nuts, like almonds and peanuts, can be a quick energy boost as well.
  • Listen to a song that gets you tapping your feet or humming along.
  • Call a friend and socialize for a bit. Getting words flowing can revitalize your brain!
